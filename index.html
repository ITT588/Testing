What is Body Mass Index (BMI)?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a way to find out a person's ideal weight range. This method uses the weight value and the height value to calculate the BMI value.

BMI is derived from the result for weight (in kilograms) with height (in meters) of a person. However, not all people can calculate their ideal weight using BMI. As examples of pregnant women, bodybuilders or athletes with high levels activity are a group of people who don't fit measured with BMI. This indicates that although their BMI value is above normal, they are does not reflect that they have excess fat and poor health.

BMI Category

BMI is calculated by dividing body weight (in kilograms) by body height (in meters or centimeter) squared. The results of BMI calculation will be divided into 6 categories, which are:

Underweight

BMI Range: 18.4 and below
Health Risk: Malnutrition risk

Normal Weight

BMI Range: 18.5-24.9
Health Risk: Low risk

Overweight

BMI Range: 25-29.9
Health Risk: Enchanced risk

Moderately Obese

BMI Range: 30-34.9
Health Risk: Medium risk

Severely Obese

BMI Range: 35-39.9
Health Risk: Malnutrition risk

Very Severely Obese

BMI Range: 40 and above
Health Risk: Very high risk
